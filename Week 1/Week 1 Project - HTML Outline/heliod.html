Deck Breakdown and Strategies

Rusko, Clockmaker: A Commander Built on Tempo

Heliod, Sun-Crowned presents a challenge to opponents on three fronts:

  1. He slots into a deck that's completely focused on gaining as much life as possible
  2. Most Heliod decks are built around agressively swinging every turn and stacking +1/+1 counters on cheap, fast creatures
  3. Heliod himself is a 5/5 indestructible body that usually activates on turn 3 or 4

To pilot Heliod effectively, plan to dump your hand as early as possible and get as many sources of lifegain on the board as possible, to start fueling Heliod's counter generation trigger. This deck wants to build an early advantage, spot remove any massive threats, and outlast and overwhelm the opponent.

Card Highlights

Midnight Clock

Midnight Clock starts slow, gaining "hour counters" each turn. In a Rusko deck, its true power shines. Rusko's ability puts counters on the Clock faster, accelerating the inevitable. When it strikes 12, shuffle your library for a massive hand refill, perfect for fueling your final push for victory.

Cyclonic Rift

Cyclonic Rift is a versatile instant in Rusko's toolbox. For just one blue mana, you can bounce a single problematic artifact or enchantment your opponent controls. But the real strength lies in its "overload" cost. Pay six extra blue mana, and Cyclonic Rift becomes a board wipe, bouncing every nonland permanent your opponents don't control. This can cripple their defenses or development, giving you a huge tempo advantage. Rusko's artifact ramp helps you reach that overload cost faster, making Cyclonic Rift a backbreaking play for your opponents to deal with.

Bolas' Citadel

Bolas' Citadel offers explosive card advantage by letting you cast spells from the top of your library by paying life, fueling Rusko's non-creature spell synergies. It also technically reduces the cost of any non-creature spell cast from the top of your deck by 1, since Rusko will drain the opponent for each non-creature spell cast, no matter how it was cast. However, its high mana cost and life payment demand careful planning and a deck with ways to gain life or mitigate life loss.

Optional Include: Paradox Engine

Paradox Engine hits the field surprisingly early in a Rusko deck, and since you'll have at least one non-land source of mana (Midnight Clock) it immediately provides value on your next turn. In most cases, you'll be getting two mana per turn out of it, and in the late game it can swing entire games if paired with Bolas' Citadel. Tap all of your non-land mana generators, then cast a spell from the top of your deck for life, which lets you untap, etc.
